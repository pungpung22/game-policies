Privacy Policy

Effective and last updated: July 20, 2026

pungpungstudi explains here how Boom Booth (com.pungpungstudi.boombooth) handles information.

Summary: No account is required. Progress is stored on the device. Firebase provides limited product analytics, crash diagnostics, Remote Config, and trusted server time. The game does not use advertising identifiers for tracking.

1. Local data

Stage progress, Gold, Hearts, item counts, tutorial state, Heart regeneration timing, and sound or vibration settings are stored on the device. The game does not directly collect your name, email, password, contacts, photos, precise location, camera, or microphone data.

2. Firebase

Firebase Analytics records product interactions such as sessions, stage attempts and results, shop views, and item purchases made with virtual Gold. Crashlytics may process crash logs and app, OS, device, and recent game-state diagnostics. Remote Config supplies balance and update values. Realtime Database server time helps reduce device-clock manipulation.

Firebase may process an app-instance identifier, approximate region derived from IP address, and app, OS, device, and network information. We do not assign a name, email, account ID, or free-form player text. Advertising storage, advertising user data, and ad-personalization consent remain denied.

3. Purchases and advertising

If in-app products are offered, Apple App Store or Google Play processes payment. We do not receive full card details. Product identifiers, purchase status, and transaction identifiers may be exchanged to deliver a purchase. The current build does not use personalized advertising or cross-app tracking.

4. Retention and choices

Local data remains until app data is cleared or the app is removed. Google and Apple retain information under their own policies and applicable law. We do not sell personal information.

5. Children and changes

The game is not designed to intentionally collect personal information from children under 13. We will update this page and store disclosures when material features, SDKs, or legal requirements change.
